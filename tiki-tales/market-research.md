# Tiki Tales — Market Research

*Date: 2026-03-07*

## Tonies Global Performance
- Founded: 2016 (Germany)
- Revenue: $500M+/year (2024)
- Tonieboxes sold: 7M+
- Figurines sold: 90M+
- Average figurines per household: ~13
- Markets: Germany, UK, US, France, Australia
- **Not available in Africa, Asia, South America**

### Tonies Pricing
| Product | Price (USD) | Price (KSh equivalent) |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| Toniebox Starter Set | $100 | ~13,000 |
| Single Tonies figurine | $15-20 | ~2,000-2,600 |
| Creative Tonie (record your own) | $12 | ~1,560 |

### Why Tonies Works
- 90M figurines ÷ 7M boxes = **13 figurines per household average**
- Each figurine is $15-20 at 70%+ margin
- Revenue per household: $100 (box) + $195-260 (figurines) = **$295-360 lifetime value**
- Figurines drive 60%+ of total revenue

## Africa Opportunity

### Market Size
- Sub-Saharan Africa: ~500M children under 14
- Kenya: ~17M children under 14, ~7M aged 3-8
- Nigeria: ~80M children under 14
- Addressable market (Kenya, middle class, 3-8): ~500,000-1,000,000 families

### Why Africa Has No Equivalent Product
1. **Import economics** — Tonies at $100 + shipping + duty = KSh 15,000+. Unaffordable.
2. **Content** — All Tonies content is Western (Peppa Pig, Disney, Frozen). Zero African stories.
3. **Languages** — Tonies supports English, German, French. Zero African languages.
4. **Distribution** — Tonies has no Africa distribution or retail presence.

### Competing Products in Kenya (Children's Audio)
| Product | Price | Limitation |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| YouTube on parent's phone | Free | Screen time, needs internet, not age-appropriate |
| Cheap Bluetooth speakers | KSh 500-2,000 | No content, no kid-friendly UX |
| LeapFrog toys | KSh 5,000+ (imported) | English only, not locally relevant |
| Storybooks | KSh 200-800 | Requires reading ability, no audio |

**Gap: No screen-free, offline, audio storytelling device with African content exists.**

### Kenya Education Context
- **CBC (Competency Based Curriculum)** requires mother tongue instruction in early years
- **Oral tradition** is central to African culture — storytelling is how knowledge was passed for centuries
- **Tiki Tales digitizes oral tradition** while keeping it physical and screen-free
- Schools need CBC-aligned content in local languages — no tools exist

### Parent Spending on Children (Kenya)
- Books/stationery: KSh 2,000-5,000/term
- Educational toys: KSh 500-3,000 (when available)
- Screen-based learning apps: KSh 0-500/month
- **Willingness to pay for "helps my child learn"**: High
