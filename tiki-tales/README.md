# Tiki Tales — African Folktale Speaker

**"Give them stories, not screens."**

A 3D-printed figurine you place on a speaker base to play African folktales and stories in any language. Screen-free, offline, built for kids aged 1-8.

## Key Takeaways

### Why This Product
- Tonies does $500M+/year globally but doesn't sell in Africa
- Reuses ~70% of KidConnect v2 hardware (ATmega328P + DFPlayer Mini + BQ24075)
- 3D printed figurines ARE the product — max leverage on our printing capability
- Every African language = new content, same hardware. Infinite scalability.
- Parents already buy stories for kids. No new behaviour to teach.
- **Reddit validated**: Screen-free is a mainstream parenting movement, not a niche

### Reddit Research Highlights (200+ parent comments analyzed)
- **Bedtime is the #1 use case** — parents buy audio players for sleep routines first
- **Price is the #1 complaint** about Tonies ($15-20/figurine). Our KSh 500 (~$4) kills this.
- **"Make your own" content is the #1 requested feature** — Creative figurine is a must at launch
- **Figurines beat cards for under-3s** — tactile, simpler, kids can use from 12 months
- **Gift economy drives growth** — grandparents, aunties buy figurines as gifts
- **Daily use confirmed** — parents report Tonies/Yoto used "every single day" for 2-4+ years
- See `reddit_insights.md` for full analysis

### The Razor & Blades Model
- Base unit at KSh 3,500 (59% margin) — gets the device in homes
- Figurines at KSh 500 each (85%+ margin) — kids collect them, parents keep buying
- Creative figurine at KSh 500 — blank NFC tag, parent loads custom MP3s
- Language packs at KSh 500 (90% margin) — swap SD card content
- School sets at KSh 12,000 (71% margin) — 1 base + 20 figurines

### Product Ecosystem
1. **KidConnect** — Literacy (phonics, reading, spelling)
2. **Tiki Tales** — Stories, culture, language preservation
3. **Math cards** — Numeracy (KidConnect expansion pack)
- Same customer. Same schools. Same brand.
