## 3. Material Strategy

### 3.1 Why PLA (Not PETG) — For Now

| Factor | PLA | PETG |
|--------|-----|------|
| **Price per spool (Kenya)** | KES 3,500 | KES 5,200+ |
| **Ease of printing** | Very easy — low temps, good bed adhesion, minimal warping | Harder — higher temps, stringing issues, needs tuning |
| **Suitable for** | Decorative & light-functional home items | Heavy-duty functional items, outdoor use, moisture exposure |
| **Print failure rate** | Low | Higher (especially for beginners) |
| **Available in Kenya** | Widely available (Jumia, Nerokas, Pixel Electric, Sangyug) | Limited stock, fewer color options |

**Decision: Start with PLA.** Our product line is decorative and light-functional (phone stands, desk organizers, planters). PLA is perfectly adequate, 49% cheaper, and easier to print consistently — meaning less wasted filament on a tight budget.

**When to switch to PETG (Phase 3+):** Once profitable and experienced, switch high-use items like wall hooks and bathroom accessories (soap dishes) to PETG for better long-term durability.

## 4. Color Strategy

### 4.1 Kenya Interior Design Trends (2025–2026)

Based on research from Kenya's leading interior design firms, the current trends driving consumer preferences are:

- **"Quiet Luxury" minimalism** — clean lines, neutral palettes, multi-functional furniture
- **Biophilic design** — indoor plants, natural tones, organic shapes
- **Afro-Modern aesthetic** — African motifs with contemporary design, earth tones
- **Bold accent pieces** — terracotta, emerald green, jewel tones for statement items
- **White/off-white dominance** — most modern Kenyan homes have white walls, making neutral decor items universally appealing

## 5. Market Research Summary

### 5.1 Kenya Home Decor Market

Kenya's home decor market is growing rapidly. On Jiji.co.ke alone, there are **123,000+ active listings** in the home accessories category in Nairobi. The strongest demand categories include:

- Decorative accessories (vases, planters, ornaments)
- Functional home items (organizers, holders, hooks)
- Affordable items under KES 1,000 (the bulk of sales volume)

Price distribution on Jiji: 6,375 listings under KES 500, 25,500 listings between KES 500–1,000. This confirms our sweet spot.

### 5.2 Why NOT Viral TikTok Products

We initially considered products trending on TikTok globally (articulated dragons, gaming controller holders, fidget toys). However, these are **poor fits for the Kenyan market** because:

- **Articulated dragons/fidgets** — Novelty items. Kenyan consumers are price-conscious and prioritize function over novelty. Limited repeat purchase potential.
- **Gaming accessories** — Niche audience. Not enough volume to justify production.
- **Seasonal Western decor** — Low cultural relevance locally.

### 5.3 Why Practical Home Items Win Here

Kenyan consumers respond to products that:

1. **Solve a real, everyday problem** (phone keeps falling, desk is messy, no hooks in the kitchen)
2. **Are priced under KES 1,000** (sweet spot for impulse/low-consideration purchases)
3. **Look good in photos** (for Jiji and Instagram listings)
4. **Are hard to find locally** in unique designs (differentiator vs. mass-produced plastic)

## 6. Lead Products — Detailed Analysis

We've selected two specific free MakerWorld designs as our launch products. Both have been reviewed against the Kenyan market and competitor pricing.

### 6.1 LEAD PRODUCT: Minimalistic Phone Stand

**Source**: [MakerWorld Model #470013](https://makerworld.com/en/models/470013-minimalistic-phone-stand)
**Designer**: Community (free download)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Description** | Clean, minimalistic universal phone/tablet stand |
| **Filament per unit** | ~25–35g |
| **Print time** | 15–30 minutes |
| **Supports needed** | No — prints flat, very easy |
| **Production cost** | ~KES 100–130 (filament + electricity) |
| **Print difficulty** | Very Easy |

#### Why This Design Specifically

- Minimalist aesthetic matches Kenya's "quiet luxury" interior trend
- No supports = zero wasted filament, near-zero failure rate
- 15–30 min print time = we can produce 20+ per day
- Universal fit — works with any phone brand

#### Competitor Pricing in Kenya

| Competitor | Price | Material | Notes |
|-----------|-------|----------|-------|
| **Jabali.ke** (Kenyan brand) | KES 300–600 | Laser-cut wood | Our closest competitor — Made in Kenya, animal/cultural designs |
| Jiji generic imports | KES 100–500 | Cheap plastic/metal | Low quality, no unique design |
| Jumia phone holders | KES 200–800 | Mixed | Mostly imported, slow delivery |

#### Pricing Strategy

| Scenario | Sell Price | Cost | **Profit** | **Margin** |
|----------|-----------|------|-----------|-----------|
| Aggressive (quick volume) | KES 300 | 130 | 170 | 57% |
| **Sweet spot (recommended)** | **KES 400** | **130** | **270** | **68%** |
| Premium (styled photography) | KES 500 | 130 | 370 | 74% |

**Recommended price: KES 400.** This undercuts Jabali.ke's wooden stands (KES 600) while maintaining strong margins. Below KES 300 we'd be competing with KES 100 Chinese imports — a race to the bottom we don't want.

#### Why This Is Our FIRST Print

The phone stand isn't our highest-margin product, but it's the **fastest path to a first sale** because:

1. **Everyone needs one** — universal demand, not niche
2. **Under KES 500** — impulse buy territory, no long decision process
3. **It's its own advertisement** — sits on someone's desk at work, colleagues ask "where did you get that?"
4. **15-minute print time** — we can produce inventory fast

#### First Sale Playbook
1. Print 3 phone stands in black (~100g total, ~1 hour)
2. Keep one on your own desk — conversation starter
3. Give one free to a friend at a busy office (marketing investment, not charity)
4. Sell one for KES 400 to someone who sees the first two
5. That free one generates 2–3 paid orders within days — office workers talk

### 6.2 LEAD PRODUCT: Modular Stackable Desk Organiser & Pen Holder

**Source**: [MakerWorld Model #1470721](https://makerworld.com/en/models/1470721-modular-stackable-desk-organiser-pen-holder)
**Designer**: Community (free download)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Description** | Modular, stackable desk organizer with pen holder — modules can be combined |
| **Filament per unit** | ~70–90g per module |
| **Print time** | 2–4 hours per module |
| **Supports needed** | Minimal to none |
| **Production cost** | ~KES 260–330 per module (filament + electricity) |
| **Print difficulty** | Easy to Medium |

#### Why This Design Specifically

- **Modular = repeat customers.** Customer buys one module, comes back for more to expand their setup.
- Stackable design means it works in any desk space — small or large
- Professional look suitable for office environments
- Pen holder functionality is universally needed in Nairobi's large office market

#### Competitor Pricing in Kenya

| Competitor | Price | Material | Notes |
|-----------|-------|----------|-------|
| Jiji basic pen holders | KES 40–300 | Cheap plastic/metal | Generic, no design appeal |
| Jiji multifunctional organizers | KES 500–900 | Plastic with features | LCD clocks, calendars — different category |
| Rio Gift Shop (Nairobi) | KES 1,349–7,499 | Wood/leather | Premium market, corporate gifts |
| House of Leather Kenya | KES 200–300 | Basic holders | Low-end market |

#### Pricing Strategy

**Per module (recommended approach — sell individually):**

| Configuration | Sell Price | Cost | **Profit** | **Margin** |
|--------------|-----------|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Single module (entry price)** | **KES 500** | **300** | **200** | **40%** |
| 2-module set (value bundle) | KES 800 | 600 | 200 | 25% |
| 3-module set (premium) | KES 1,100 | 900 | 200 | 18% |

**The modular upsell strategy:**
- Sell the first module at KES 500 (low entry price gets the customer in)
- Offer additional modules at KES 400 each ("add-on" pricing feels like a deal)
- Customer builds their perfect setup over time = recurring revenue from the same buyer

This is more powerful than selling fixed sets because:
1. Lower entry barrier (KES 500 vs KES 1,100 for a full set)
2. Repeat purchases — they come back to expand
3. Print on-demand instead of guessing quantities
